# Stay Safe Online: Cybersecurity Checklist for Students

College life in India is increasingly digital, from fee payments to online classes and social networking. This checklist provides specific, actionable steps to protect your digital identity and finances from prevalent cyber threats.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety

* **Never Use PIN to Receive Money:** A UPI PIN is only required to *deduct* money from your account. If an app, buyer, or person asks for your PIN to "send you a refund," "deposit cash," or "test a transaction," it is an immediate scam.
* **Beware of QR Code Scams:** Fraudsters often send QR codes over WhatsApp, Telegram, or online marketplaces (like OLX) promising cashback, festival discounts, or advances for items you are selling. Scanning these codes and entering your PIN will instantly drain your funds. Always verify the merchant directly.

---

## Social Media & Identity Protection

* **Lock Down Your Profiles:** Keep your Instagram, Facebook, and other personal social accounts private. Cybercriminals frequently scrape photos from public profiles to create fake accounts, morph images, or target your friends and family with "emergency distress" scams asking for money.
* **Scrutinize Job and Internship Offers:** Be highly suspicious of unsolicited messages offering lucrative part-time jobs, "work from home" data entry gigs, or internships that demand an upfront "registration fee," "laptop fee," or "security deposit." Legitimate companies do not ask candidates to pay for employment.

---

## Device & Network Security

* **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking:** Campus Wi-Fi, hostel networks, and free café connections are often unencrypted or easily spoofed, making it easy for attackers to intercept your data. Always use your mobile network or a trusted Virtual Private Network (VPN) when accessing banking apps, paying university fees, or logging into sensitive portals.
* **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Protect your primary email, university portals, and social media accounts by enabling 2FA. Even if your password is compromised in a data breach or stolen via a phishing link, the secondary OTP or authenticator app will keep attackers out.
* **Audit Your App Permissions:** Flashlight apps do not need access to your contacts, and calculator apps do not need access to your photo gallery. Restrict permissions, especially for unverified loan apps or third-party APKs downloaded outside the official Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Malicious apps can steal contact lists to extort or harass students.
* **Sanitize Shared Drives:** College students frequently share USB drives or external hard disks for notes, projects, and media. Regularly scan these external devices with an updated antivirus program to prevent ransomware, spyware, and malware infections from spreading to your primary laptop.

---

## Emergency Reporting Channels

> **Act Fast:** If you fall victim to cyber fraud, the "Golden Hour" is critical. Reporting financial fraud immediately significantly increases the chances of authorities freezing the stolen funds before they are withdrawn.
>
> * **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** File an official, detailed complaint online at [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
> * **National Cyber Helpline:** Dial **1930** immediately to report financial fraud. This connects you directly to the authorities who can liaise with banks to halt fraudulent transactions.
